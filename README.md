# **A lot of pollock is fished in Alaska. Almost none stays there.**
*looking into how much pollock leaves Alaska... and its a lot*

## Alaska's resources are obviously bountiful, and as a result extensively exploited. 

### I was curious *who* was moving fish out of Alaska and *where* it was going. And, a lot of the vessels shipping out pollock use predatory FOCs, or "flags of convenience," a growing issue in global maritime trade. 
###Using ImportGenius data I was able to get a better picture of the route pollock takes once it leaves Alaska. The data is surely incomplete, since much doesn't even get registered to a port before leaving (and ImportGenius uses port registration to track shipments).


### The social and economic problems caused by resource exploitation go hand in hand with environmental disregard. The ecosystem in the Bering Sea is under great duress — very likely only becoming more and more dire — as a result of bottom trawling.
